CV

I am a PhD student in the School of Neuroscience at Virginia Tech, where my research focuses on the molecular mechanisms of thermoreceptors in Drosophila. I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Biology at the University of Washington, Seattle. My work involves using a combination of cellular and molecular techniques, CRISPR, electrophysiology, immunohistochemistry, and behavioral experiments, to investigate the mechanisms underlying temperature sensation. I am expected to graduate in 2027 and am currently seeking a collegiate faculty position.

I am the recipient of the F31 Predoctoral Fellowship (Ruth L. Kirschstein NRSA Individual Predoctoral Fellowship) from the National Institutes of Health (NIH) to support my dissertation research on temperature sensation in Drosophila. Additionally, I have published in high-impact journals such as Nature, Current Biology, and the Journal of Neuroscience.

My work has been recognized with several awards, including First Place at the Center for Communicating Science Nutshell Games at Virginia Tech, the Kirk and Noel Schulz Transformative Graduate Education Fellowship, and the Howard Hughes Medical Institute Integrative Research Award during my time at the University of Washington. I have also received several travel awards for presenting my research at conferences.
